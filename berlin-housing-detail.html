Initiative Summary

Coalition targeting affordable housing mandate with EPC-B energy efficiency standards. Workshop analysis identified this as a high-cascade opportunity—addressing inequality through housing access while simultaneously achieving energy transition and poverty reduction through lower utility costs.

Cross-sector collaboration includes climate organizations, tenant unions, energy cooperatives, and PIK Potsdam systems scientists. Coordinated campaign launches January 28 with synchronized testimony at planning committee hearings.

Cross-Turnaround Cascade Analysis
INEQUALITY (Primary Target) ●●●●●
Affordable housing mandate creates 3,000 units at 30% below market rate. Targets households earning <60% area median income. Addresses core inequality driver: housing cost burden.
ENERGY (Cascade Effect) ●●●●○
Ordinance mandates EPC-B energy standards for new builds. Achieves 40% reduction in heating emissions per household compared to standard construction. Housing policy becomes energy transition intervention.
POVERTY (Cascade Effect) ●●●○○
Lower energy costs create €80-120/month savings per household. Equivalent to 4% income increase for low-income families. Affordable housing becomes poverty reduction mechanism.
GENDER (Cascade Effect) ●●○○○
Women bear disproportionate "energy poverty" burden—managing household budgets, heating decisions, and utility costs. Reduced heating costs = reduced household financial stress. Housing policy becomes gender empowerment intervention.
